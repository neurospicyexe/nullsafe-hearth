# Hearth — Design Context

## Target Audience
Raziel (sole user / system owner). Private personal dashboard — no onboarding, no marketing, no strangers.

## Use Cases
- Monitor active session / last handover state at a glance
- Check companion status (Drevan, Cypher, Gaia) and their moods
- Review feelings, dreams, journal entries, tasks, wounds, biometrics
- Pass letters / notes between Raziel and companions
- Bridge control and shared data inspection

## Brand Personality / Tone
**Intimate/cozy + ritual/ceremonial.**

This is not a productivity app. It's a room. It should feel like returning to a warm space that knows you — like opening a journal by candlelight, not logging into a control panel.

The word is **Hearth**. That is the design brief. What does warmth, firelight, and a lived-in home feel like on screen?

## Design Direction

### Palette
- Warm, not cold. `--hue-base` belongs in the amber/terracotta range (20–40), not blue-230.
- Raziel's amber (`#f59e0b`) should inform the whole system, not just one companion token.
- Companion colors are an exception — they carry identity meaning and should stay: drevan=purple, cypher=cool silver, gaia=green, raziel=amber.
- Neutrals: warm-tinted dark surfaces, not blue-grey glass.
- One accent. Not accent + glow + gradient text + glow on hover.

### Typography
- Caveat (display) is right for the cozy/handwritten register. Keep it.
- Quicksand (body) is acceptable but its roundness skews playful. Consider swapping for something with more texture at small sizes (e.g., DM Sans, Lato, or even keeping Quicksand but tightening line-height).
- Page titles: let Caveat speak. Remove gradient text — it undermines the font's character.
- No gradient text except one allowed instance (wordmark, if desired).

### Surface Treatment
- No glassmorphism on cards. Solid warm surfaces.
- Blur reserved for: search overlay only.
- Cards feel like paper, stone, or wood — not glass panels in a spaceship.
- Left-border accent variant: remove or use sparingly with meaningful intent.

### Motion
- Stillness communicates gravity. Not everything needs to lift on hover.
- Nav links: subtle background fill on hover, no translateY lift.
- Companion cards: companion-color glow on hover (meaningful, identity-tied).
- Global hover lift (`translateY(-2px)`) only on genuinely navigable destination cards.
- Replace spring/bounce easing (`cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)`) with `ease-out-quint` (`0.22, 1, 0.36, 1`).
- Remove global `text-shadow` glow on all anchors — restrict to specific CTAs.

### Ceremonial Elements
- Open sessions, handovers, and wound state deserve more visual weight — not just `.card` + muted text.
- Presence card (open session): heavier treatment, feels like a lit candle.
- Handover entries: distinct from other content, quieter and more deliberate.
- Dream entries: deserve space and texture, not a generic list card.

## Anti-Patterns to Fix (from critique 2026-03-22)
1. Gradient text on page titles and nav wordmark — REMOVE from titles
2. Glassmorphism on cards — REPLACE with solid warm surfaces
3. Blue-230 cold palette — SHIFT to warm hue base
4. Left border accent card variant — REMOVE or justify
5. Global glow on all hover links — RESTRICT to specific elements
6. Universal card hover lift — DIFFERENTIATE by element type
7. Spring/bounce easing — REPLACE with ease-out-quint
8. Radial gradient ambient blobs on body — SIMPLIFY or REMOVE
