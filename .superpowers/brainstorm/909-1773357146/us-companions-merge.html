Merging Us + Companions — which structure feels right?

All three put companion cards and feelings on the same page. The difference is how they're organised and how deep you go before a "see more".

A

Us page absorbs Companions

The /us page becomes the one home for everything relational. Top: love meter + active anchor. Then three companion cards (mood + click → /companions/id). Then 5 recent feelings with a "see more →" clip. Then letters inbox, handovers, journal. /companions list page goes away entirely.

Pros

  • One page to rule it all
  • Companions feel integrated, not isolated
  • Removes a dead-end list page

Cons

  • Us page gets long
  • Nav needs updating
B

Home page absorbs Companions, Us stays focused

Companion mood cards on the home page (already there) get clickable names linking to /companions/id. /companions list page goes away. /us keeps its current content but is trimmed and better-spaced. This is the lightest-touch option.

Pros

  • Minimal surgery
  • Home already has companion cards
  • Us page stays focused on emotional content

Cons

  • Companions only visible at home — buried if you're on another page
  • Doesn't really "merge" the pages
C

New "Together" page replaces both Us + Companions

Rename /us to /together (or keep the path, change the title). Companion cards at top with mood + link. Love meter + anchor strip. Then a tabbed or sectioned feed: Feelings · Letters · Journal · Handovers — each section shows 5 items then "see more". /companions list page is retired.

Pros

  • Clean fresh feel, not a patch job
  • Tabbed feed prevents the page from feeling overwhelming

Cons

  • More work to build
  • Tabs add interaction complexity

Also in scope — applies to whichever option you pick

  • Search/filter bar on every major list page (feelings, journal, handovers, tasks, dreams)
  • 5-item clip with "see more →" instead of infinite scroll throughout
  • Home page spacing fix (more breathing room between sections)
